Frequently Asked Questions

What is Proxy, and how is it different from traditional robots?

Proxy is a new branch of robotics - any robot designed for remote real-time use by a human. The first Proxy will be a modular robot, offering a wide range of applications from exploration and entertainment to practical tasks in various environments.

How will I use a Proxy?

The Proxy Terminal is the device that will allow humans to remotely connect to their Proxy, providing an immersive and interactive experience.

Will I need special skills to operate a Proxy?

We're designing Proxy to be easy to use, so you won't need any special skills to operate one. If you can ride a bike, you can pilot a Proxy. We're committed to making this technology accessible to you.

How far will I control my Proxy from?

The only real limit to the range of Proxy control is the speed of light. Beyond that, any constraints are just engineering challenges that we're actively making progress on.

Our mission is to extend your reach as far as possible.

Will I be able to customize my Proxy?

Absolutely! We intend for Proxy to be both modular and customizable, ensuring it feels like a true extension of yourself. We also plan to open-source the Proxy software, so other creators can build upon it and create their own custom Proxy.

How much will Proxy cost?

While prices are subject to change, we hope to offer the first Proxy robots for roughly $1000, with the Proxy Terminal around $500. We want Proxy to be affordable, without compromising on quality or performance.

When can I buy a Proxy?

We're hard at work on our first interactive prototype, and plan to have it ready by August 2024. We're optimistic that Proxy will be available for purchase sometime in 2025, and will provide more specific timelines as we get closer to launch.
