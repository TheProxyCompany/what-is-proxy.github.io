Frequently Asked Questions

How is Proxy different from traditional robots?

Unlike traditional robots designed for automation or pre-programmed tasks, Proxy is specifically built for real-time human control. This means you directly pilot the robot, experiencing the world through its sensors and making decisions in the moment.

How do I use Proxy?

The Proxy Terminal is the device that will allow humans to remotely connect to their Proxy robot, providing an immersive and interactive experience.

Do I need special skills to operate a Proxy?

Absolutely not! We're designing Proxy to be intuitive and easy to use. If you can ride a bike or play a video game, you can pilot a Proxy. Our goal is to make this technology accessible to everyone.

How far can I control my Proxy from?

The range of control is virtually limitless, thanks to high-speed internet connectivity. You can pilot your Proxy from anywhere with a stable internet connection, regardless of geographical distance.

Can I customize my Proxy?

Definitely! We believe your Proxy should be an extension of yourself. Our robots are designed to be modular and customizable, allowing you to personalize its appearance and functionality to suit your needs and preferences. We also plan to open-source the Proxy software, and we will encourage others to build their own custom Proxy.

How much will Proxy cost?

While final pricing is still being determined, our goal is to make Proxy affordable and accessible. We hope to offer the first Proxy robots for ~$1500, and the Proxy Terminal for ~$500. We are making Proxy affordable, without compromising on quality or performance.

When will Proxy be available?

Our team is actively developing the first Proxy prototype and is on track for a demo in Summer 2024. We will announce more specific timelines for product availability as we get closer to launch.
